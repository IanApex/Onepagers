What is Faceted Search?

It's a system that lets users narrow down results by applying multiple filters (facets) at once. It turns an overwhelming catalog into a manageable list of relevant options.

Common Design Mistakes

Avoid these common pitfalls that create frustration instead of clarity.

Showing Every Filter

Overwhelming users with dozens of options at once defeats the purpose.

No Result Counts

Users filter blind, often leading to "0 results found" dead ends.

Hidden Applied Filters

Users lose track of their selections and can't easily modify them.

Design Best Practices

Follow these principles to create a filtering experience that feels helpful, not complicated.

Progressive Disclosure

Show the top 3-5 filters by default, with a "Show More" option to prevent initial overwhelm.

Mobile Best Practices

  • Use an overlay or slide-out panel for filters.
  • Ensure controls are large and touch-friendly.
  • Let users select multiple filters, then tap an "Apply" button to see results (batch application).
  • Show a summary of applied filters on the results page.

The Bottom Line

Good faceted search feels like a knowledgeable assistant guiding users to the perfect option. It transforms overwhelming choice into a powerful, user-controlled discovery tool.

The goal isn't to show every possible filter—it's to help users find what they want as efficiently as possible.
